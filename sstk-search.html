    Overview

    Shutterstock connects customers to the content they need, so they can create their next big idea. As part of the search team, we strived to innovative the search experience. In a typical search, a user enters their query and then lands on a search results page. Ours was no different.

    Continue reading to find out how we identified the specific focus area in the search journey and what solutions we produced.

    Research

    Through funnel analyses and other data points, we discovered the search traffic declining steadily quarter-by-quarter by roughly 3-5% in major categories such as paid order rate, search usage and signups. Our customers were searching less and abandoning due to not finding what they want. We investigated further through user research. The focus was to understand the user's search behavior, goals within searching, and pain points throughout the search journey.

    RESEARCH ACTIVITIES

    RESEARCH FINDINGS

    • The main problem we uncovered was users constantly yo-yoing back-and-forth between tweaking their search and scrolling through results over and over again because they couldn't find what they are looking for and ultimately abandon.
    • Surprisingly, users barely used filters to refine their searches.
    • Over 70% of user's first action from the search results page after scrolling through the results was clicking on the search bar to edit their query.
    • Users generally have a vague idea of what they are looking for but they don’t know how to describe it.

    We identified that the beginning of the flow lies the biggest area of opportunity. The specific spot we identified was between clicking the search bar and landing on the search results page.

    How might we present a more explorative and refined view to reduce the amount of back and forth and ultimately guide the user to exactly what they are looking for?

    DESIGN WORKSHOP

    I facilitated a 3 day design studios workshop with our search team where we took the time to understand the problem through data and research we prepared beforehand. During our synthesis, we found a clear winning problem to solve for along with secondary problems that we documented to follow up on later.

    We placed the most voted ideas within a priority tree with the main idea at the top along with secondary ideas below it.

    OUTCOME

    The specific problem we clearly wanted to focus on was at the beginning of the journey. If we can provide a more refined view of categorized assets before landing on the unorganized search results page, the user will find their asset sooner reducing the number of revisits to the search bar.

    User testing results

    After many internal discussions and design critiques, I ran the design through usability testing to ensure the experience was worth building and to garner a sample size of user sentiment towards the new experience. The method I used was unmoderated interviews via UserZoom externally and moderated with internal teams due to time constraints.

    Learnings

    Overall, users had an overwhelmingly positive response with the addition of an empty state that conveniently contained their past history. For the top refinements screen, participants expressed words such as unique, helpful, “gives them an edge in seeing more, better images”, and useful. I incorporated all of the top feedback into the final designs.

    Below is one specific learning that had to be addressed going back to what I previously mentioned about how users first click on the search bar after scrolling.

    Question:
    Where else would you click to go back to the "Top refinements" screen?

    Because a user's first inclination is to click back on the search bar to perform another search, I thought it made sense to have them return to the top refinements this way but majority of the participants had low confidence in this task and it showed with a 0% success rate. They were close but they did not click where we expected them to.

    The obvious solution that we went with was adding a "Back to top refinements" button in addition to clicking the search bar to return to the new experience that made it very clear on how to go back to the previous view if they wanted to explore a different option.

    Engineer handoff

    Our engineer team was involved starting from participating in the design workshop to reviewing wireframes and providing feedback on feasibility to design handoff. We were in constant communication and I provided extensive documentation of the functionality, spacing, and all of the edge cases which there were many for this new experience.

    I mapped out each edge case and user flow clearly indicating how a user would navigate the experience.

    Dealer market exchange

    ×

    My role

    Design lead

    Duration

    1 year

    Activities

    UX/UI design, user research

    Platform

    Mobile app, SaaS

    Overview

    Dealer Market Exchange was creating the car industry's first business mobility solutions platform. They had finished building its core features in its enterprise solution for automotive businesses. There focus turned to launching their online auction platform to allow users to conveniently bid on vehicles with a few clicks.

    I was tasked with creating their notification system initially for auctions but futureproof it for other features launching later.

    How might we help users receive timely notifications and immediately take action when participating in an online auction?

    RESEARCH

    Research methods

    With this being my first assignment, I immediately started creating a research plan to gain a better understanding of who the users are and their behaviors. I explored the car buying experience especially through auctions. I interviewed buyers at a live auction in-person and asked about their roles and their experience doing business with other dealerships.

    The insights were a little shocking with how antiquated their processes were but made sense for over a century old industry.

    RESEARCH FINDINGS

    • Everything is still done on paper such as processing inventory and purchasing a vehicle from another dealer
    • Like anyone, dealers are much more comfortable staying within their network or acquaintances when conducting business rather than brand new clients.

    In addition to my user research plan, I gathered competitive takeaways. I looked up many popular apps that do notifications well like social media apps to gain a greater understanding of the mechanics of notifications.

    PRINCIPLES

    After compiling all the research, I formed principles that influenced the designs to satisfy users' needs.

    • Design a UI that allows for quick actions on auctions
    • Consistency between web and mobile
    • A scalable UI to integrate other notification types in the future

    EXPLORATIONS

    Once I felt I had everything I needed, I began sketching different ideas that came into my head from the culmination of all the research I did. Below are some of the examples that I'm able to show.

    Investments tab

    Investment options

    I explored different formats and possible iterations such as having a larger icon to visually indicate at a glance which type of notification it is.

    WIREFRAMES

    After iterating on paper and reviewing with my manager, I was able to narrow down the concepts and start converting the sketches into wireframes in Sketch. After multiple rounds of iteration and review, I converted the designs into higher fidelity using our design system.

    Iteration progression - oldest to newest

    Icon-centric, glanceable

    Smaller icons, easy access to actions

    Text based, simplified

    Time emphasized, primary CTA

    FINAL DESIGNS

    The "time emphasized, CTAs grouped" concept was chosen due to the badges containing key pieces of information a user needs at a glance for time critical auctions along with a clear call-to-action.

    • Utilized existing elements and brand colors
    • Emphasis on important information
    • Every notification has a follow-up action

    • Allow for more time to conduct deeper user research to better guide design decisions
